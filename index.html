About History

Key Milestones in the Development of LLMs

1960s: Joseph Weizenbaum developed ELIZA, an early chatbot using pattern matching to simulate conversation.

1990s: RNNs were created for sequence data but had limitations with long sequences, leading to LSTM networks.

2014: GRUs emerged as a simplified version of LSTMs, while attention mechanisms enhanced focus on key information.

2017: Transformers revolutionized text processing with parallel analysis, improving context understanding.

Since 2018: BERT and GPT models have advanced natural language processing through bidirectional information flow.

Explore Application Scenarios

What Can Large Language Models Help Humans Achieve?

Customer Service

When you need answers from customer service without the wait, LLMs provide instant responses, available 24/7.

Writing Assistance

Struggling to start a report or an article? LLMs can generate content and even check for grammar and spelling errors.

Data Analysis

For business professionals and researchers, LLMs can quickly parse complex data and reports, offering insights that would otherwise be difficult to extract.

Smart Search

Often, online searches fail to yield the desired results. LLMs can understand your queries more accurately and provide more relevant responses.

Learning and Education

LLMs can serve as educational aids, helping both students and educators to learn more effectively.

Medical Diagnosis

In the medical field, LLMs can assist doctors by interpreting medical records and test results, even offering preliminary diagnostic suggestions.
